A5/A7 Infographic

The data set used for this infographic describes natural disasters in counties around the world. To this aim, I created two charts to describe this data. In one, a time series is shown tracking global deaths attributed to natural disasters over the years. Two frames of reference are given for this time-series, one from the years 1900-1950 and the other from 1970-2015. The difference between the two highlights the marked decrease in deaths due to natural disasters recently when compared to the turn of the 20th century. Also in this visualization is a bar chart simply describing the tota; frequency of natural disasters over the 120 years this data set covers. This bar graph gives a comparative tool in which to compare the instances of a specific disaster to the deaths it caused, showing which natural disasters are dangerous because they happen a lot and which are deadly every time they strike. The other visualization is a heatmap representing each individual country and the effect of natural disasters per 100k people. This metric gives a normalized measure of how natural disasters have affected each country within each decade from 1900-2020. Together, these graphs give a summed effect of specific natural disasters on the world over time and a summed effect of every natural disaster on specific countries over time.
